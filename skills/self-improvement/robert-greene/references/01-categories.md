# Category: Authority and Presence

> Laws that govern **how you are seen** and how that perception gives you power.

The laws in this category operate on **public image**. They most directly build your **authority capital** — the recognition that opens doors before you open your mouth.

## Why presence matters

- People decide about you in the **first 7 seconds**
- Authority bias is one of the strongest in social psychology
- Physical, vocal, and narrative presence can **multiply** your message
- Without presence, talent is invisible

## The 8 laws in this category

| # | Law | Key phrase |
|---|-----|------------|
| 1 | Never outshine the master | Make your boss shine |
| 2 | Never put too much trust in friends | Be more dangerous than you seem |
| 3 | Conceal your intentions | Keep people in the dark |
| 4 | Always say less than necessary | Silence is power |
| 6 | Court attention at all cost | Be seen, be heard |
| 14 | Pose as a friend, work as a spy | Similarity builds trust |
| 28 | Enter action with boldness | The ordinary makes you ordinary |
| 34 | Act like a king | Posture is destiny |

## Productive tension

These laws carry an **internal tension**:

- **Be visible** (Law 6) vs **conceal intentions** (Law 3)
- **Imitate the boss** (Law 14) vs **never outshine the master** (Law 1)
- **Show effort** (Law 30) vs **act like a king** (Law 34)

Learning to navigate these tensions is the mark of the mature strategist.

## Daily application

| Context | Priority law |
|---------|--------------|
| Meeting with superiors | 1, 14 |
| Initial negotiation | 3, 4 |
| Project launch | 6, 28 |
| Public crisis | 34, 46 |

# Category: Strategy and Timing

> Laws that govern **when you act** and **how you deploy your energy**.

Power is not won through brute force — it is won through **timing and direction**. The laws in this category teach you to **wait for the right moment**, **choose your battlegrounds**, and **not disperse your forces**.

## Why strategy matters

- Time is the most scarce and democratic resource
- Acting at the wrong moment converts victory into defeat
- The concentrated beat the dispersed
- Patience is the highest form of power

## The 11 laws in this category

| # | Law | Key phrase |
|---|-----|------------|
| 9 | Win through your actions | Demonstrate, don't persuade |
| 13 | Appeal to self-interest | Goodwill exhausts itself |
| 15 | Crush your enemy totally | Learn from the best |
| 16 | Use absence | Engineer scarcity |
| 17 | Control the territory | He who dictates the field dictates the battle |
| 22 | Use the surrender tactic | Preserve the power to return |
| 23 | Concentrate your forces | Master one point |
| 29 | Plan all the way to the end | 90% of success is execution |
| 31 | Control the options | Less is more |
| 35 | Master the art of timing | Don't have time — create it |
| 45 | Beg for something big | He who asks for a million gets half a million |

## The strategic triangle

```
     TIME
      /\
     /  \
    /    \
ACTION-STRUCTURE
```

- **Time** (Laws 16, 22, 29, 35): when you act
- **Action** (Laws 9, 28, 45): how you act
- **Structure** (Laws 17, 23, 31): where you act

All three vertices are necessary. Neglect one and you collapse.

## Category mantra

> *"The patient do not die. The impatient do."* — Sufi proverb

# Category: Reputation and Perception

> Laws that govern **the story you tell about yourself**.

Reality matters less than the **interpretation** of reality. The laws in this category teach you to build and protect **your public narrative** — how you are seen, what is remembered, which flaws are forgiven, and which wins are magnified.

## Why reputation matters

- It is the **cognitive shortcut** that precedes every relationship
- It takes years to build; an instant to destroy
- Your personal narrative **filters** which information reaches you
- Personal brand is professional reputation converted to an asset

## The 7 laws in this category

| # | Law | Key phrase |
|---|-----|------------|
| 5 | So much depends on reputation | Guard it with your life |
| 25 | Re-create yourself | Renew or die |
| 26 | Keep your hands clean | Confess before being caught |
| 30 | Make accomplishments seem effortless | Easy success is suspicious |
| 37 | Create compelling spectacles | What is seen is remembered |
| 40 | Despise the free lunch | The gift poisons |
| 46 | Never appear too perfect | Calculated imperfection breeds affection |

## The central paradox

> **Be imperfect in form, perfect in substance.**

People want to admire your **work** and feel **close** to you. If your work is mediocre but your brand is polished, you look like a fraud. If your work is excellent but your brand is cold, you look unreachable. The balance is art.

## Practical rules for building reputation

1. **Consistency**: the same message across all channels for years
2. **Selective visibility**: show up for the moments that matter, not all of them
3. **Measured vulnerability**: a beloved flaw is worth more than ten wins
4. **Long time**: reputation is built in decades, not months
5. **Active defense**: attack gossip early, don't wait for it to set

## Reputation audit

Each year, ask yourself:
- What three words would people use to describe me?
- Is there a false rumor circulating that I have not corrected?
- Is my public narrative aligned with my internal narrative?
- Have I neglected any front (LinkedIn, networks, team)?

# Category: Relationships and Alliances

> Laws that govern **how you connect** with others to build power networks.

No one reaches the top alone. The laws in this category teach you to **build, maintain, and use relationships** as power infrastructure.

## Why alliances matter

- Your **network is your most stable asset**
- Information flows through relationships, not org charts
- The right alliances multiply your capacity
- The wrong alliances destroy you before you see it coming

## The 7 laws in this category

| # | Law | Key phrase |
|---|-----|------------|
| 7 | Get others to do the work, take the credit | Cede the spotlight |
| 8 | Use absence to increase respect and value (use carrot, not stick) | Reward makes you owner |
| 11 | Learn to keep people dependent | Independence impresses but does not protect |
| 12 | Use selective honesty and generosity | Double hook |
| 20 | Do not commit to anyone | Stay free to switch sides |
| 27 | Create a cult-like following | Sell meaning |
| 41 | Avoid stepping into a great man's shoes | Never be vassal to one lord |

## Alliance types by depth

| Type | Depth | When to use |
|------|-------|-------------|
| Mentor | High | When you need judgment |
| Peers | High | When you need real allies |
| Colleagues | Medium | When you need a professional network |
| Clients | Medium | When you need reputation |
| Acquaintances | Low | When you need general information |
| Watched enemies | Low | When you need to know what is happening |

## The art of giving and receiving

The economy of relationships is not 50/50 — it is **wavelike**. Sometimes you give more, sometimes you receive more. What matters is the **long-term trend**: if you always give and never receive, you are exploited; if you always receive and never give, you are isolated.

## Five cardinal rules

1. **Never break a pact without notice** — surprise is worse than betrayal
2. **Do not mix money and intimate friendship** — it converts what was emotional into transactional
3. **Be the first to trust** — initial vulnerability generates reciprocity
4. **Cultivate who can help you** before you need them
5. **Cut toxic relationships fast** — one toxic ally damages you more than 10 enemies

# Category: Self-Defense

> Laws that govern **how you protect yourself** from the power of others.

Not everyone has good intentions. The laws in this category teach you to **detect threats**, **defend without self-destruction**, and **maintain your integrity** in a world that constantly wants to take it from you.

## Why self-defense matters

- Persistent naivety is an invitation to abuse
- Those who cannot say "no" cannot say "yes"
- Personal freedom is a **daily practice**, not a permanent state
- Predators detect their victims by **unconscious signals**

## The 6 laws in this category

| # | Law | Key phrase |
|---|-----|------------|
| 10 | Avoid the unhappy and unlucky | They are social poison |
| 18 | Do not build fortresses | Isolation is dangerous |
| 19 | Know who you're dealing with | Do not offend the wrong person |
| 42 | Strike the shepherd and the sheep will scatter | He who pays, commands |
| 44 | Disarm and poison with the illusion of kindness | The wolf in sheep's clothing |
| 47 | Do not go past the target | Excess is the father of disaster |

## Types of aggression to recognize

| Type | Signal | Defense |
|------|--------|---------|
| Passive-aggressive | "It's nothing, I'm just saying..." | Direct, calm confrontation |
| Emotional manipulation | "If you loved me, you'd do X" | Set clear boundaries |
| Workplace harassment | Constant and targeted | Document, escalate, leave |
| Social isolation | You are excluded from meetings | Build your own external network |
| Gaslighting | They deny what is evident | Document everything in writing |

## Active self-defense techniques

1. **The invisible shield**: don't react in the moment; process later
2. **The mirror**: return the criticism as a question ("why do you say that?")
3. **The long pause**: 10 seconds before responding to a provocation
4. **The kind yes**: "Yes, you're right that..." (disarms without yielding the point)
5. **The elegant exit**: leave before the situation escalates

## The golden rule of self-defense

> *The best fight is the one you don't have. The second best is the one you win fast. The worst is the one you win slowly.*

If you can avoid a fight, avoid it. If you cannot, end it fast. If you cannot end it fast, reconsider whether you should be in it.

# Category: Psychology and Shadow

> Laws that govern **the invisible part of the game**: your own mind and others' minds.

The deepest laws are the ones no one sees operating. The laws in this category teach you to **understand the unconscious mechanisms** that move people — including yourself. He who understands others' shadow sees in the dark.

## Why psychology matters

- 90% of human decisions are **unconscious**
- Emotions are the gasoline, reason is the steering wheel
- Your own biases are traps you don't see
- Self-awareness is the **only sustainable competitive advantage**

## The 9 laws in this category

| # | Law | Key phrase |
|---|-----|------------|
| 21 | Play a sucker to catch a sucker | Show 60% of your capacity |
| 24 | Play the perfect courtier | Become the role |
| 32 | Play on people's fantasies | Sell dreams, not products |
| 33 | Discover each person's weakness | He who knows your wound owns you |
| 36 | Disdain things you cannot have | Make that indifference real |
| 38 | Think as you like but behave like others | Be strange in private; conventional in public |
| 39 | Stir up emotions to maximize effect | In cold you calculate; in hot you decide |
| 43 | Work on the hearts and minds of others | Stories + reasons |
| 48 | Assume formlessness | Be water, my friend |

## The most relevant cognitive biases

| Bias | What it does | Ethical use |
|------|--------------|-------------|
| **Anchoring** | The first number sets the scale | Open with the first offer |
| **Confirmation bias** | We see what we want to see | Present your idea as confirmation of theirs |
| **Loss aversion** | Pain of losing > pleasure of winning | Frame what they gain by NOT acting |
| **Social proof** | We do what others do | Show similar cases before asking |
| **Scarcity** | Rare seems valuable | Limit time, quantity, access |
| **Reciprocity** | We return what we receive | Give first, without conditions |
| **Authority** | We obey who seems expert | Project competence through subtle signals |

## The Jungian shadow

Carl Jung spoke of the **shadow**: the parts of you that you repress. People in power have enormous shadows — ambition, cruelty, selfishness. **Don't deny them**: integrate them. A recognized shadow is fuel; a repressed shadow explodes.

## Recommended inner work

1. **Journaling** 10 minutes daily: what did I feel today? what did I avoid feeling?
2. **Meditation** 10 minutes: observe thoughts without reacting
3. **Reading classics**: psychology, philosophy, history
4. **Therapy or mentorship** at least once a year
5. **Honest feedback** from 3 trusted people: what do I not see about myself?

## Ethical warning

> The laws in this category are the **most easily abused**. A master of psychology without a moral compass becomes a pure manipulator. Every technique must pass the filter: *does this genuinely help the other, or only me?*
