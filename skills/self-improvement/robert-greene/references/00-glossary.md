# Glossary of Terms

> Recurring vocabulary in the 48 Laws.

## A

**Ally**
A person with whom you share **interests** at a given moment. Does not imply eternal loyalty. An ally can be temporary, conditional, or permanent.

**Leverage**
The capacity to **multiply your effect** by using resources that do not belong directly to you. An idea with leverage is an idea with funding, allies, and momentum.

**Appearance**
The **how** of your presence. Includes dress, posture, language, gestures. Distinct from "essence" (what you really are). Good appearance without essence is empty; good essence without appearance is invisible.

## B

**Banking role**
A person who **lends resources** and obtains influence in exchange. In modern politics, central banks, investment funds, and patrons are bankers in the broad sense.

**Goodwill**
A feeling of affection and willingness to help without guarantee of reciprocity. **Volatile** by nature. Laws 8, 11, 13, 20 emphasize that it is not a stable basis for alliances.

## C

**Social chameleon**
Someone who **adapts their behavior** to the group without losing their inner essence. Laws 14, 24, 38.

**Charisma**
Combination of physical presence, voice, narrative, and timing that **elevates your influence** beyond what your formal position allows.

**Conviction**
**Deep and operative** belief in something. Distinct from opinion. He who has conviction moves; he who has opinions argues. Law 24.

**Cult (strategic sense)**
An emotionally bonded community around a **shared idea, practice, or identity**. Law 27.

## D

**Defensive (ethical level)**
Application of the laws to **not be deceived**, not to deceive. Level recommended to start.

**Productive disdain**
**Worked** indifference toward the unattainable. Law 36. Not cynicism; it is emotional self-sufficiency.

**Cognitive discrepancy**
The mismatch between what you believe and what you do. People **detect** it and distrust. Law 24.

## E

**Essence vs Appearance**
- **Essence**: what you are when no one sees you
- **Appearance**: what you project
- Law 34: make appearance match or exceed essence
- Law 46: make appearance be **slightly below** essence

**Status**
Objective social position (title, wealth) plus the **perception** of that position. Laws 5, 14, 34 revolve around how it is built.

## F

**Focus**
Concentration of energy on **one thing** until mastered. Law 23.

**Key phrase (of each law)**
The **crystallization in one phrase** of the principle. Designed for memorization and to invoke the law in the moment.

## G

**Emotional hook**
Message that **captures attention** by appealing to a deep desire, fear, or aspiration. Law 32.

**Gaslighting**
Manipulation that makes the other **doubt their own perception**. Not a law from the book, but Law 33 warns about its detection.

**Conflict management**
Set of techniques to **handle disputes** without self-destruction. Laws 19, 22, 47.

## H

**Selective honesty**
Telling **chosen truths with strategic purpose**, not indiscriminate truths. Law 12.

**Strategic withdrawal**
Temporary retreat to **conserve power** or increase mystery. Laws 16, 22.

## L

**Dark side**
**Manipulative or harmful** applications of the laws. Not recommended. See [ethical line](../SKILL.md#the-ethical-framework-mandatory) in SKILL.md.

**Law**
In this context: **observed principle** about how power works, **not moral commandment**. The laws describe; they do not ethically prescribe.

## M

**Perfect master**
Person who has already **mastered the field** and can teach judgment, not just technique. Law 15.

**Mechanics (of a law)**
The **psychological or social explanation** of why the law works. It is the hidden "engine."

**Mystery**
State of **incomplete information** about your intentions, capabilities, or plans. Laws 3, 4, 16, 21.

## N

**Deal (broad sense)**
Any **exchange of value** between people. A friendship is an emotional deal. Law 13.

**Neutrality**
State of **non-commitment** to sides in dispute. Law 20.

## P

**Leverage (exploitation)**
**Fulcrum** from which to apply minimum force for maximum effect. The laws describe where the levers are.

**Power**
**Capacity to influence** others so they act according to your will, not necessarily by force.

**Posture**
**Physical and emotional** state that projects dignity, calm, or authority. Law 34.

**Principle**
The **theoretical essence** of each law, expressed in an abstract phrase.

## R

**Reciprocity**
Psychological impulse to **return** what we receive. Powerful but dangerous if it is the only basis for alliance.

**Renewal (of identity)**
**Deliberate and deep** change of how you define yourself. Law 25.

**Reputation**
**Accumulated public perception** of your character and competence. Cognitive shortcut. Law 5.

## S

**Social sanction**
Informal punishment or reward that a group applies to those who **break or respect** its norms. The laws operate on this mechanism.

**Seduction (broad sense)**
**Maintained attraction over time**. Not just romanticism. Laws 12, 27, 32, 39.

**Shadow (Jungian)**
The **repressed parts** of your personality. The laws recommend recognizing it, not projecting it. Laws 33, 48.

## T

**Tactic vs Strategy**
- **Tactic**: **individual** and concrete action
- **Strategy**: **plan of consistent tactics** toward an objective

**Puppeteer**
He who controls **without appearing**. Law 7.

**Trap (of a law)**
The **risk or contraindication** of misapplying the law. Each law in this repo includes its own trap.

## V

**Perceived value**
What someone **believes something is worth**. Can be distinct from real value. Laws 5, 16, 30, 40 work on this.

**Vassal**
He who depends on **a single lord**. Vulnerable position by definition. Law 41.

## Z

**Carrot vs Stick**
- **Carrot**: reward → generates **motivated self-interest**
- **Stick**: punishment → generates **obedient fear**
- Law 8 recommends carrot by default.
