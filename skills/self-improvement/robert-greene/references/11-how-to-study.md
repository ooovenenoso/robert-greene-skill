# How to Study the 48 Laws of Power

> A methodological guide to get the most out of this repo — regardless of your prior level.

## Before starting: the right question

> **Do you want to understand power, or dominate others?**

- If you want to **understand**: this repo is for you.
- If you want to **dominate without ethics**: this repo is not for you.

The laws are descriptions of how the world works. **How you use them defines who you are.**

---

## 4-level study method

### Level 1 — Comprehension reading (4–6 weeks)

**Objective**: understand what each law says

1. Read the [SKILL.md](../SKILL.md) in full
2. Read the 48 laws in order, **one per day**
3. For each law: read it twice, once slowly
4. Note **one personal application** per law
5. Review the [one-line summary](../SKILL.md#the-48-laws--quick-index) at the end

**Time**: 20–30 minutes/law = ~24 hours total

### Level 2 — Active memorization (4–6 weeks)

**Objective**: invoke the law in the moment

1. Memorize the **key phrase** of each law (use flashcards)
2. Associate each law with **one historical figure** (use [historical-figures](09-historical-figures.md))
3. Review by category, not by number ([categories](01-categories.md))
4. Invent your own example per law (more solid than Greene's)

**Card technique**: front = situation, back = applicable law

### Level 3 — Reflective application (3–6 months)

**Objective**: use the laws with judgment

1. At the end of each day, **record 1 moment** where a law was in play (for or against you)
2. **Identify which law you used without knowing its name**
3. Practice **one new law each week** in a real context
4. Keep an **application journal**: law, context, result, learning
5. Discuss the laws with a friend or mentor: defended arguments stick more

### Level 4 — Ethical mastery (1 year or more)

**Objective**: integrate the laws as a system

1. Study the [map of laws](../SKILL.md#key-tensions-what-makes-this-skill-non-obvious) in depth
2. Identify the **tensions running through** your professional life
3. Work with a mentor or therapist on **your own shadows**
4. Read **complementary works** in [bibliography](10-bibliography.md)
5. Teach the laws to others: the best way to consolidate learning

## Three ways to approach the study

### By **number** (linear)
- Good for: beginners, initial reading
- Time: 8–12 weeks
- Pro: covers everything; con: loses thematic context

### By **category** (thematic)
- Good for: those looking for immediate application
- Time: 4–6 weeks
- Pro: connects similar laws; con: loses narrative flow

### By **problem** (situational)
- Good for: professionals with a concrete need
- Time: per need
- Pro: direct application; con: fragmented understanding

## Common mistakes when studying the laws

| Mistake | Why it's a problem | Solution |
|---------|--------------------|----------|
| Wanting to apply them **all at once** | Paralysis; each law has its context | Master 3–5 first, expand later |
| Applying them as **recipes** | You lose adaptation to context | Read the mechanics, not just the phrase |
| Using them for **revenge** | It destroys you inside | Apply the ethical filter |
| Memorizing without **experimenting** | Dead knowledge | Keep the application journal |
| Judging each law **morally** | Some laws describe, not prescribe | Distinguish description from prescription |

## Deepening rituals

### Morning ritual (5 min)

1. Read one law at random from the compendium
2. Ask yourself: **how does it apply TODAY in my life?**
3. Note one application intention

### Weekend ritual (15 min)

1. Review the week's journal
2. Identify the **most present law** and the most ignored
3. Plan **one action** for the next week aligned with a chosen law

### Monthly ritual (1 h)

1. Read a complete category
2. Make a **mind map** of the laws and their connections
3. Write a short essay on what that category taught you

### Annual ritual (1 day)

1. Re-read the complete repository
2. Identify which **laws dominate** your current life
3. Choose **3 laws to master** the next year

## To share and teach

If you will teach these laws (articles, courses, conversations):

1. **Cite the original** — always mention Robert Greene
2. **Distinguish** what is your reinterpretation from what is from the book
3. **Accompany with ethics** — never teach only the dark side
4. **Tell stories** — people memorize narratives, not lists
5. **Ask first** — "Which law have you seen operate in your life?"

## Verify your learning

Periodically ask yourself:

- [ ] Can I cite the 48 laws from memory with the key phrase?
- [ ] Can I give a personal example for each one?
- [ ] Can I identify which law each person around me uses?
- [ ] Can I explain the **tensions** between complementary laws?
- [ ] Can I say **when NOT** to apply each law?
- [ ] Have I read at least 2 complementary works?
- [ ] Have I applied at least 10 laws in real contexts?

If you answered yes to **5+ questions**, you are on the right path.
