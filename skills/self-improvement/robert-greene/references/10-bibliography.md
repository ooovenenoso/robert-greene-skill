# Bibliography

> Sources, complementary readings, and works cited or related to Las 48 Leyes del Poder.

## Primary work

### Robert Greene — *The 48 Laws of Power* (1998)

- **Original publisher**: Viking Penguin (United States)
- **ISBN**: 978-0-14-028019-7 (Penguin paperback edition)
- **Structure**: 48 chapters (one per law) + prologue + bibliography
- **Style**: narrative, with historical anecdotes from antiquity to the present
- **AUDIENCE**: this repo is **inspired by** and reorganizes the work — not a copy

## Classical sources cited

### Greco-Roman antiquity

- **Thucydides** — *History of the Peloponnesian War* (5th c. BC) — political realism
- **Plutarch** — *Parallel Lives* (1st-2nd c.) — Greene's main source of anecdotes
- **Polybius** — *Histories* (2nd c. BC) — doctrine of mixed government
- **Xenophon** — *Cyropaedia* and *Anabasis* — education of leaders and expeditions
- **Suetonius** — *Lives of the Twelve Caesars* (2nd c.) — biographies of Roman power
- **Tacitus** — *Annals* (2nd c.) — power under the empire

### Chinese and Eastern thought

- **Sun Tzu** — *The Art of War* (5th c. BC) — foundational text of strategy
- **Mao Tse-tung** — *On Protracted War* (1938) — war and patience
- **I Ching** (millennial classic) — change and timing

### Italian Renaissance thought

- **Machiavelli** — *The Prince* (1532) — the closest work in spirit
- **Machiavelli** — *Discourses on the First Decade of Titus Livius* (1531)
- **Castiglione** — *The Book of the Courtier* (1528) — the art of presence
- **Boccaccio** — *Decameron* (1353) — narratives of social power

### Modern thought

- **Baltasar Gracián** — *Oráculo manual y arte de prudencia* (1647) — key for Greene
- **La Rochefoucauld** — *Maximes* (1665) — aristocratic cynicism
- **Cardinal de Retz** — *Memoirs* (1717) — 17th c. conspiracies
- **Friedrich Nietzsche** — *Beyond Good and Evil* (1886) — will to power
- **Max Weber** — *Politics as a Vocation* (1919) — the three types of power

## On ethical application

> The 48 Laws are controversial. If you want a **critical or ethical** reading on power:

- **Rollo May** — *Power and Innocence* (1972) — humanist critique
- **Hannah Arendt** — *On Violence* (1970) — distinction power/violence
- **Chögyam Trungpa** — *Cutting Through Spiritual Materialism* (1973) — shadow and power
- **Robert Caro** — *The Power Broker* (1974) — biography of Robert Moses, urban power
- **Henry Kissinger** — *On China* (2011) — political realism in practice

## Note on rights

This repository is a **reinterpretation** structured around the conceptual content of Greene's work. All textual content here is **original to this repo**. For the full narrative versions with detailed anecdotes, consult Robert Greene's original book, which we strongly recommend.
